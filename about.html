About Me

I am Jintian YAN, a graduate student in Artificial Intelligence at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology. I have always been deeply interested in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and wish to gain a deeper understanding of user-centered design principles and their practical applications. In the context of the rapid development of AI, I am also eager to explore how artificial intelligence can assist in designing interactive systems, enhancing both intelligence and user experience. Through this semester’s HCI course, I hope to systematically learn the core principles, methods, and tools of interaction design, and understand how interactive systems can meet real human needs in today’s society, especially in the evolving post-pandemic context. I aim to develop the ability to design systems that balance technical feasibility with user experience, laying a solid foundation for future work in AI-assisted design or user experience optimization.

Background

Biomedical engineering, health informatics, and AI-related design contexts.

Interest

HCI, AI systems, emotional support technology, and design research.

Goal

Develop a portfolio that records both design outcomes and thinking process.
